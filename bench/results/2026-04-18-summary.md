## What each scanner actually found

### Pencheff — 22 verified, business-impact findings

Ruled out 31 SPA-404 / informational-noise findings via the AI triage
pass before reporting. Every remaining finding has reproduced HTTP
evidence, CVSS, and CWE.

**High (2):**
1. **No HTTPS / cleartext transport** — CVSS 7.5, CWE-319
2. **Web cache deception** — `GET /nonexistent.css` serves authenticated
   content; cacheable → attacker can expose cached sensitive data.
   CVSS 7.5, CWE-525

**Medium (6 headline):**
- No account lockout / rate limit on `/login` (verified with 10 failed
  attempts). CVSS 7.5, CWE-307
- Weak password policy — registration accepts `"password"`. CVSS 7.5,
  CWE-521
- PostgreSQL port 5432 reachable externally. CVSS 6.5, CWE-284
- `X-HTTP-Method-Override` honoured — potential access-control bypass.
  CVSS 5.4, CWE-650
- `/api/Challenges` 500s on `__proto__` prototype-pollution payload.
  CVSS 5.3, CWE-20
- Multiple rate-limit gaps on `/register`, `/forgot-password` (30
  rapid requests accepted). CVSS 5.3, CWE-770

Plus 8 other medium findings (TLS / HSTS / CSP headers, OWASP A05),
4 low, 2 informational.

**Executive summary from the assessor's note**:
> "The application exhibits two unmitigated high-severity findings —
> absence of HTTPS transport encryption and a web cache deception
> vulnerability — that collectively preclude a grade above C.
> Additionally, fourteen medium-severity issues were identified,
> including weak password acceptance, missing rate limiting on
> authentication endpoints, an exposed PostgreSQL port, and multiple
> missing security headers (HSTS, CSP)."

Every alert is a `WARN-NEW` passive observation:

## What the scoreboard doesn't say

Juice Shop challenges have **specific completion detectors** baked
into the application (e.g. `/rest/admin/users` flips
`"Admin Section"`, a particular base64 in the `/rest/user/login`
query flips `"Login Admin"`). Triggering them means actually executing
the exploit end-to-end, not merely observing that it's possible.

Automated scanners rarely solve many Juice Shop challenges without a
human in the loop. Even the agent-driven Pencheff solved 0 (the 2
tallied are auto-solves). **Where Pencheff meaningfully differs from
ZAP is in surfacing verified, audit-ready findings with
evidence and priority — not in the raw scoreboard number.**

## Honest caveats

- We ran **ZAP baseline**, not `zap-full-scan.py`. A fair full-scan
  run would take ~45 min and surface more (mostly noise). Update the
  runner script and re-benchmark before publishing.
- Pencheff's agent is **non-deterministic** — a second run will find
  18–27 findings, not the same 22 every time. For a published number,
  average N ≥ 3 runs and show the standard deviation.
- The LLM cost figure assumes Claude Haiku 4.5 at current pricing
  (~$1/$5 per 1M tokens). It's real money, though not much.
